Polaris Pool Robot

The two DC motors for the robots back wheels with controller board

Over the summer, I found someone giving away this free robot because it had trouble starting in the pool. The original owner had just replaced the motor block that you see in the above photo, however the robot still would not run. I began my research into how the robot is supposed to function by reading the manual and checking power levels across the main power board. The power output was correct, so I began to suspect that there could be an issue with communication between the motherboard in the power unit and the daughter board on the robot. I used an oscilloscope to check the output of the transceiver on the motherboard and it was pulsing with consistency. My next step was to check the outputs through the waterproof cable that attaches the robot to the power supply and motherboard. Unfortunately, I had to return to school before I could make any more progress, but I am excited to continue work on this robot in the summer.
