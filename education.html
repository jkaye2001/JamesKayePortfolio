Foundations of Robotics Fall 22'

The Simulated Maze Environment for our Final Project

One of my favorite classes so far. Foundations of robotics allowed me to finally explore one of my passion fields. I have been a fan of robotics ever since I took my first robotics course in high school. In this course we learned about many different types of robots, sensors, and movement methods. For the last month of the semester, we were given a final project to have an autonomous robot complete a maze that it had no prior knowledge of. This was all done over simulation using Python, Ubuntu, ROS and the Gazebo simulation environment with one of their robot designs. Our team came to the idea that a wall following robot using LIDAR would be the best solution for this problem, since it could solve any maze we came up against. On the day of class, we all put our simulations to the test and had a fun competition before finals.

Mobile Computing Fall 22'

A Display of my Dark Mode and System Preferences Settings

As a professional elective, I took Mobile Computing last fall and learned about how to develop mobile applications for iPhones. At the end of the class, we worked in groups to come up with our own idea for an app and build it. My app idea was a workout app that would provide a 3D model of proper form for a list of different exercises. I recruited two of my friends to assist me with the design and we were able to incorporate a few exercises, a list of health articles and some bonus features like the addition of dark mode that I coded myself above.
